"Coin"


Money-saving mobile application The project requirement was about creating a money-saving tool OR online-shop. I chose the first one to make variety in my experience. However I decided to combine both concepts - money-saving application with a small online-store, where any user can buy tools for helping them to save money (piggy-bank, wallet, notebooks etc.)!


Idea

Creating mobile money-saving tool, that would include also an online shop with items that help to save money.

My Role

  • Finding the idea
  • Creating the concept
  • Researching and analyzing
  • Finding user problems and possible solutions
  • Designing the app
  • Prototyping
  • Testing
  • Iterating
  • Implementing the final design

The 5W method helped to confirm the app's idea and the concept.


Design Evolution


The key points of the app's development, starting with building a cohesive brand, ending with the final high-fidelity wireframes and mockups.


User Flow Diagram


The diagram defined key user-flows. Due to the project requirements and limits, I have covered a few of the key-user flows.


Paper sketches and low-fidelity wireframes


Paper sketches and prototypes, as well as low-fidelity wireframes show the user-flow tasks and the way's of it's implementation. Defined app's structure was changed and iterated in order to ease the cognitive load.


1. Mid-fidelity wireframes and 2. Mid-fidelity wireframes after an analysis and iterations


My next two steps of the development - iteration of the the mid-fidelity wireframes. I split some of the screens into a few, to make the user's tasks more clear and easier to complete.


Building a cohesive brand and First High-fidelity wireframes


For the cohesive brand I used light, minimalistic and elegant style. Becoming rich is a beautiful process :)! The app's logo has round abstract shape and represents coin. Color palette has yellow as an accent color, which represents gold, optimism and energy.


High-fidelity wireframes after multiple iterations


Above you can see the final high-fidelity wireframes with the flows noted above each.


Desktop Landing page


Next pace was designing the desktop landing page. The building picture perfectly fits the app's style, supports the concept and evokes security feeling from the users. I used minimalistic design, geometric patterns and light, elegant decorations.


Desktop Mockup



Mobile Mockups


Designed mockups for this application help us to see how this application would look in the real life.


Conclusion


Went well:

Implementing the project brief, combining the ideas (money-saving and online-store app), creating user-flow, researching, generating the cohesive brand, finding the style and creating the UI elements, decorating the app, aligning with layouts and the grids.


Challenges:

Correcting the user-flow, making additional screens in order to reduce a cognitive load, choosing the key-flow for screen development.


Experience and further plans:

Practice more layout and the grid implementation, white space incorporation, design hierarchy and UI element's grading.
